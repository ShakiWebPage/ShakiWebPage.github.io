Material and cultural heritage
Material and spiritual heritage of Sheki

Architectural monuments include Sheki Khan's Palace, Shakikhanov's House, Gilakli Mosque Minaret, Mosque, Fortress Walls, Sheki Round Temple (XVII-XIX), Zayzid Temple (XI-XII), Kish Albanian Temple of the nearby Kish village, Sheki Caravanserai. In order to distinguish the specific features of the planning of the ancient city, it is important to study the general construction of city streets and the architecture of Sheki. Unlike other medieval cities in Azerbaijan, which have gradually built streets, this work in Sheki was carried out in a short time and in a planned manner. The main and longest street of Sheki, parallel to Gurjanchay, contains the most important architectural monuments of the city. Many streets of Sheki attract attention with the presence of historical and architectural monuments.






Castle
Nukha Castle

The fortress is located in the higher part of the city, in the north-east and surrounds Sheki khan's palace. The fortress is located not only in military-strategic terms, but also in a very favorable microclimate. The total length of the castle walls is 1300 m, the wall on the north side is 4 m high, and the wall on the south side is 8 m high. The thickness of the castle walls is 2.2 m. The castle has two gates on the south and north sides and towers for protection. Today, despite the restoration work carried out in 1958–1963, the castle walls and towers are in a state of disrepair. According to the plan mentioned on February 26, 1853, it was planned to restore about 40 historical monuments in the fortress.

Sheki Kalagayi

Kalagayi is a rectangular headdress made of silk thread for women in Azerbaijan. In the western part of Azerbaijan, kalagai is sometimes called chargat. In the late 19th and early 20th centuries, Azerbaijan's traditional kalagai centers changed their position for various political and socio-economic reasons, especially as a result of the displacement of cheap factory goods from Russia by artisanal quarry products. In 2014, the future was included in the Representative List of Intangible Cultural Heritage of UNESCO.
