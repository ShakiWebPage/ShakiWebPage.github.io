Farhadbeyovs' House

It is located in the upper part of the city, on M. Huseynzadeh Street. It occupies a special place among the monuments built after the flood in the XVIII-XIX centuries. The owner of the house was Farhad Bey. The yard area is 600 square meters. There is a melon and a shed in the yard. The thickness of the walls of the house is 90 centimeters. The house is built of raw brick and river stone. Lime was used in its construction. It has two floors. The height of the rooms is 2.2 meters on the first floor and 3.3 meters on the second floor. The first floor consists of three rooms, each with access to the balcony. Both rooms have a steam room, niche and shelves. The doors are double. On the second floor there are two separate rooms and four interconnected rooms. The height of the windows is 30 centimeters, there are niches for decorations at a distance of 60-70 centimeters from the ceiling. There is a balcony in the middle of the house. The balcony is decorated with wooden columns. Half of the windows in small rooms are lattice. The roof of the building is covered with tiles. The entrance located on the main facade of the house is of special interest. A staircase leads to the second floor from the arched doorless entrance. The skeletons of two goats' heads were nailed to the wall above the arch. According to ancient beliefs, this protects the family from evil and brings prosperity to the home. The house was sold in Farhad Bey's time.
