General Information
Shaki

Sheki is one of the oldest cities in Azerbaijan. It has been a republican city since January 4, 1963. In medieval sources, his name is Shaki, Sheka, Shakki, etc. called as. The city of Sheki has long been called Nukha. The powers of the head of the Sheki city executive power also apply to the Sheki region. Sheki is located in the north-west of the country, on the southern slope of the Greater Caucasus Mountains, on the left bank of the Kish River, at an altitude of 632 m above sea level, 370 km from the capital Baku, far from the central regions - in an isolated area. There are 71 settlements in the district. 1 of them is a city, 2 are settlements, 68 are villages. As a result of charming nature, unique historical and architectural monuments, developed craftsmanship, protection of rich historical and cultural heritage, Sheki has become an important tourist region of Azerbaijan.

Population

As of 01.01.2017, the population of Sheki region is 184172 people. 68,360 of them live in cities and 115,812 in rural areas. There are 309 families of martyrs, 183 invalids of the Karabakh War, 12 veterans of the Second World War in the region. 13 residents of the district were awarded the title of Honored Honor of the Republic of Azerbaijan, 1142 people are Presidential Scholars. 1764 refugees and 4949 IDPs settled in Sheki region. 86115 people are engaged in labor activity. The number of students is 29,694, the number of pensioners is 25,957.
